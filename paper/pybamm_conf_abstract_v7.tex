{\bfseries\large Cross-supplier state-of-health forecasting for second-life\\
LFP cells with a PyBaMM-trained encoder--decoder neural operator}\\[0.4em]
\underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India}\\
krishna.vaddepally@turno.club\\[0.2em]
{\footnotesize Theme: Data-driven Methods \textbullet\ Presentation preference: Oral (poster acceptable)}
\end{center}

Second-life reuse of retired lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells is
limited by the cost of characterising each cell's future ageing.
Conventional lifetime tests take months and empirical models transfer
poorly across supplier chemistries. We present a physics-conditioned
encoder--decoder neural operator that forecasts the full state-of-health
(SoH) trajectory to end-of-second-life from a compact diagnostic
snapshot plus $\sim{}50$ cycles of field cycling.

The workflow (Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline}) has three stages. Phase~1
identifies beginning-of-life electrochemistry per anchor cell from OCV,
HPPC, GITT and RPT. Phase~2 fits five degradation parameters --- SEI
growth rate, SEI molar volume, solvent diffusivity, lithium plating
rate and negative-electrode loss of active material --- by differential
evolution against measured cycling. Phase~3 runs PyBaMM~[1] with the
SPMe formulation and Prada2013 LFP chemistry~[2] to synthesise 490
perturbed trajectories around seven anchor cells across three suppliers
(CALB, EVE, REPT); a SoH-translation augmentation expands this into
14{,}096 (context,~target) pairs covering the second-life SoH regime,
teaching the operator to be translation-invariant on the absolute SoH
axis. The operator is a $\theta$-conditioned Neural ODE~[3] whose
decoder is integrated by \texttt{torchdiffeq} (dopri5) under a
softplus-monotonic constraint, and whose context encoder ingests
$K{=}50$ observed cycles of the target cell. Inference therefore
consumes three inputs: the nearest-supplier $\theta$ vector as a
physical prior, DCIR and initial-capacity features from a short
characterisation, and the $K$ observed cycles.

Held-out evaluation used one previously unseen cell per supplier
(CALB\_0029, EVE\_0003, REPT\_0031). Per-cell SoH root-mean-square errors
were 0.95, 0.83 and 0.82 percentage points respectively
(Figure~\ref{fig:heldout}), with mean $0.87$~pp and cross-supplier
standard deviation $0.06$~pp: every held-out cell fell below one
percentage point and the per-cell error was nearly chemistry-independent.
Each forecast is a genuine forward projection --- the decoder extends
roughly 500 cycles beyond the observed window into deeper second-life
SoH values. Limitations: the cross-supplier claim rests on three
held-out cells; the forecast horizon is capped by the 2500-cycle PyBaMM
corpus; beyond-window projections remain experimentally unverified.
