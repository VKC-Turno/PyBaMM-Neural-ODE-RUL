\vspace{-3.6em}
Second-life reuse of used lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) cells for
stationary energy storage is bottlenecked by characterisation cost.
Per-cell PyBaMM~[1] calibration of degradation parameters against
measured state-of-health (SoH) trajectories is reliable but requires
$\gtrsim 400$ cycles per cell~[2]; below that, the fitted rates are
under-determined. Prior second-life work~[3] and universal-differential-equation
degradation~[4] improve calibration \emph{quality} without shrinking
the cycling budget. We ask: \emph{can a physics-conditioned neural
operator, pretrained on PyBaMM-generated synthetic trajectories,
predict the full SoH curve of a new cell from a single characterisation
snapshot plus a short cycling window?}

We construct a two-stage pipeline. \emph{Stage 1 --- Physics corpus:}
starting from PyBaMM's Prada2013 LFP chemistry with per-cell BOL
parameters identified from OCV, GITT, HPPC and RPT on a
supplier-B fresh-cell cohort (OCV RMSE 6.8~mV median, GITT R$^{2}$
0.9999), we run a Sobol
sweep of 300 simulations across five degradation channels ---
$k_{\text{SEI}}$, SEI partial molar volume, lithium-plating exchange
current, positive- and negative-electrode LAM rates --- producing 300
synthetic SoH trajectories with paired ground-truth parameter vectors
$\theta$. \emph{Stage 2 --- Neural operator:} a DeepONet~[5] variant
whose branch consumes a five-stream fingerprint (DCIR profile, RPT
capacity+IC-peak features, first-$K$ measured SoH values, the PyBaMM
parameter vector $\theta$, and the cycling protocol) and whose trunk
consumes the target cycle number; the output is
$\text{SoH}(n) = \text{SoH}_{\text{init}} - \text{softplus}(\langle
b, t \rangle)$, hard-monotonic by construction. The $\theta$-conditioning
lets the operator absorb electrochemistry \emph{directly} from the
Phase-1 param-ID step rather than reconstructing it from characterisation.

Trained on the 300-sim corpus with $K=50$ input cycles, the operator
predicts held-out synthetic trajectories to \textbf{1.0~pp SoH RMSE
median} on a 15\% validation split (322k parameters, 500 epochs,
$\sim$10~s per epoch on a single GPU). The talk will present three
extensions being finalised: (i) an extended 300-sim $\times$
1500-cycle corpus so the operator sees full end-of-life shape, not
just early-window fade; (ii) fine-tuning on real cells from all three
suppliers to correct any sim-to-real residual; (iii) evaluation of whether
the operator's predictions transfer across three commercial LFP
suppliers (anonymised A/B/C) without per-supplier retraining.
