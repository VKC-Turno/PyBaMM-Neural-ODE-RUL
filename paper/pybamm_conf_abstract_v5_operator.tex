\begin{center}
{\large\textbf{From a single characterisation snapshot to end-of-life:\\
a PyBaMM-trained neural operator for second-life LFP RUL}}\\[0.55em]
\underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India}\\
\texttt{krishna.vaddepally@turno.club}
\end{center}

\vspace{0.15em}

Second-life reuse of used lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) cells for
stationary energy storage is bottlenecked by characterisation cost.
Per-cell PyBaMM~[1] calibration of degradation parameters against
measured state-of-health (SoH) trajectories requires at least 400
cycles per cell~[2] to reliably extrapolate to end-of-life; below
that, the fitted rates become under-determined. Prior second-life
work~[3] and universal-differential-equation degradation~[4] improve
the quality of the per-cell fit but do not shrink the cycling budget
itself. Reducing that budget requires transferring knowledge from a
synthetic parameter-space corpus to individual cells rather than
fitting each from scratch.

The approach has two stages: an offline physics corpus built with
PyBaMM's DFN model on the Prada2013~[2] LFP chemistry and per-cell
beginning-of-life parameters identified from OCV, GITT, HPPC and RPT
tests on a fresh supplier-B cohort; and an online neural operator ---
a DeepONet~[5] variant conditioned on the identified degradation
vector $\theta$ --- that maps a five-stream characterisation
fingerprint to the SoH trajectory at any queried cycle. Figure~1
summarises the flow.

End-to-end validation on a single supplier-B cell drives both the BOL
identification and the degradation-parameter fit from that cell's own
data. Fitting the four SEI and plating parameters against its
150-cycle measured Longterm record yields \textbf{0.3\,pp SoH RMSE};
the resulting PyBaMM long-run reaches first-life end-of-life
($\text{SoH}=0.80$) at \textbf{cycle 3921} with a smooth monotonic
fade, consistent with major LFP manufacturer datasheet cycle-life
claims at 0.5\,C. In this presentation the trained operator's
predictions on 20 held-out cells across three commercial LFP
suppliers will be shown, together with the identifiability limits of
parameter fitting from short cycling records and extensions to
non-isothermal deployment.
