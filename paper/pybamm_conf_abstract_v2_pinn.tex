\title{\vspace{-1.0cm}\textbf{\normalsize A universal physics-informed
neural network for used-LFP RUL:\\ 50-cycle characterisation across
three manufacturers, 8$\times$ cycling reduction vs pure PyBaMM}}
\author{\normalsize \underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\[-0.3em]
  \small \emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), India}}
\date{}

\thispagestyle{empty}

\vspace{-3.6em}
Second-life repurposing of used lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) cells for
stationary energy storage is bottlenecked by characterisation cost:
pack builders must estimate remaining useful life (RUL) with no
beginning-of-life data and limited cycling. Pure PyBaMM~[1]
reaction-limited SEI calibration requires ${\approx}400$ cycles per
cell for $<3$\,pp SoH extrapolation on used cells; prior work~[3,\,4]
improves calibration quality but not the cycling budget. \emph{Can one
physics-informed neural network with fixed hyperparameters cut required
characterisation across LFP manufacturers?}

We train a joint PINN across all cells in a cohort:
$\text{SoH}_\theta(n) = \text{SoH}_\text{init} -
\text{softplus}(\text{NN}_\theta)$ with hard-monotonic decrement and
per-cell latent embedding. Physics constraint:
$\text{d}\text{SoH}/\text{d}n = -k_\text{SEI}(i)\,\text{SoH}^{p(i)}$
(Ramadass-canonical rxn-limited SEI~[5]) with per-cell learnable
$(k_\text{SEI}, p)$ warm-started from a linear fit on the training
window. Loss combines data MSE, physics MSE on collocation points
across the \emph{full} cycle domain, and boundary/monotonicity
regularisation. Adam + cosine, 10k epochs, ${\approx}4$~min on a GPU.

Applied to 20 LFP cells across three manufacturers (7 MFR\_A, 9 MFR\_C,
4 MFR\_B) with \emph{identical} architecture and hyperparameters (K=50,
${\approx}68$k params): the joint PINN achieves held-out RMSE
$<3$\,pp SoH on \textbf{18/20 cells} (Fig.~1). On deep-second-life
MFR\_A cells (15--25\,pp fade over 1000+ cycles), where pure physics
fails catastrophically (median 20\,pp), the PINN maintains
\textbf{median 1.93\,pp — a 10$\times$ reduction} and beats physics
7/7 head-to-head (Fig.~2). On near-fresh MFR\_B/MFR\_C cells (1--3\,pp
fade), physics is near-optimal ($<0.4$\,pp) and PINN matches without
harm. \textbf{Versus the pure-physics K=400 baseline, the same PINN
reaches equivalent accuracy at K=50 — 8$\times$ cycling reduction with
make-agnostic transfer.}

\vspace{0.2em}
Results hold for LFP at 25\,$^{\circ}$C isothermal; cells flagged by an
upstream shape filter (non-monotonic, three-regime, batch-transition
data artefacts) remain out of scope. PyBaMM \texttt{v26.4.3}'s
\texttt{Prada2013} rxn-limited SEI rate is embedded as the physics
constraint.
