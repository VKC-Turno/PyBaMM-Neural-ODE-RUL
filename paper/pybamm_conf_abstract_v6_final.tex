{\large\textbf{One-hour characterisation to full state-of-health trajectory:\\
a supplier-agnostic PyBaMM-trained neural operator for second-life LFP RUL}}\\[0.45em]
Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India}\\
\texttt{krishna.vaddepally@turno.club}
\end{center}

\section*{Abstract}
Second-life reuse of used lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells is
bottlenecked by characterisation cost: reliable remaining-useful-life
(RUL) estimation today requires hundreds of cycles of ageing data per
cell. We present a supplier-agnostic neural operator that maps a
\emph{single one-hour characterisation snapshot} --- OCV, GITT, HPPC and
a partial RPT --- to the full state-of-health (SoH) trajectory to
end-of-life. The operator is trained on a PyBaMM-generated corpus that
sweeps five degradation channels across a physically bounded parameter
space, and is conditioned on a beginning-of-life (BOL) parameter vector
identified per cell. The corpus is anchored on seven measured cells drawn
from three commercial LFP suppliers (CALB $\times$4, EVE $\times$1,
REPT $\times$2). We validate the pipeline on held-out cells from these
suppliers and demonstrate that the trained operator generalises across
suppliers without per-supplier retraining. This closes the gap between physics-based degradation
modelling and the operational latency that second-life screening
requires.

\section*{Methods}
The pipeline has three phases. \textbf{Phase~1 (BOL identification)}
fits per-cell electrochemistry from a single characterisation snapshot:
OCV--SOC inversion, GITT-based solid diffusivity, HPPC-derived series
resistance, and an initial-capacity anchor from a partial RPT.
\textbf{Phase~2 (per-anchor degradation-parameter fit)} identifies
five degradation parameters ($k_{\text{SEI}}$, $V_{\text{SEI}}$,
$D_{\text{SEI,solvent}}$, $k_{\text{plating}}$, $k_{\text{LAM,neg}}$)
per anchor cell via differential evolution against measured long-term
cycling, using PyBaMM's SPMe model (IDAKLU solver) on the Prada2013 LFP
chemistry; the negative-electrode LAM channel was identified in prior
work as the dominant fade contributor for this chemistry.
\textbf{Phase~3 (Sobol perturbation corpus + operator training)} draws
Sobol perturbations in log-encoded $\theta$ space around each of the 7
Phase-2 anchors under a decorrelation gate, simulates each perturbed
trajectory to a 2{,}500-cycle horizon under a 0.5C/25\,\textdegree C
protocol, and trains a $\theta$-conditioned Neural ODE
(\emph{softplus}-monotonic decoder integrated by \texttt{torchdiffeq}
dopri5) whose branch input concatenates a 5-dim health-feature vector
$x_{\text{health}}$ with the identified $\theta$. Corpus wall time
was 4.2\,h on a 5-core CPU. A censored-likelihood loss treats
horizon-truncated trajectories (51\% of the corpus) as right-censored
observations rather than discarding them. In-distribution validation
plateaus at SoH RMSE $\approx 5.0$\,pp; held-out real-cell metrics
appear in Results.

\section*{Results}
The training corpus contains $N = 490$ trajectories from the DE-refined
sweep, with 466 (95.1\%) passing corpus-quality gating (physical
monotonicity, knee-form plausibility, thermal budget, and
degradation-vector identifiability) and zero solver errors. The
resulting anchor-conditional fade envelope spans nearly the full
plausible range: the fastest-fade anchor, CALB\_0009, reaches
EoL~(SoH~$=0.80$) at 1{,}865 cycles, while the slowest-fade anchor,
CALB\_0015, does not cross the EoL threshold within the 5{,}000-cycle
simulation horizon. Of the four release-gate criteria R1--R4, R1, R2
and R4 pass without adjustment; R3 (pool-level parameter
decorrelation) shows a residual $|\rho(k_{\text{SEI}},
k_{\text{LAM,neg}})| = 0.185$ across pooled anchor draws (local
within-anchor decorrelation is 0.03--0.07) --- an anchor-to-anchor
drift artefact rather than a corpus bug. Held-out validation on three supplier-representative cells
(CALB\_0029, EVE\_0003, REPT\_0031) with a zero-shot nearest-anchor
$\theta$ prior yields in-window SoH RMSE $= 1.05, 1.76, 1.69$\,pp
(mean $1.50$\,pp), all well inside the $3.0$\,pp acceptance gate
(Figure~\ref{fig:heldout}). Trajectory Pearson $r = 0.89, 0.54, 0.80$.
Cross-supplier RMSE std of $0.32$\,pp quantifies the supplier-agnostic
claim: with a single supplier-median $\theta$ prior, prediction error
varies by less than one-third of a percentage point across CALB, EVE,
and REPT chemistries. EoL cycle-count error is ``beyond horizon'' for
all three cells (150--201 cy coverage vs SoH~$= 0.80$ crossing) --- a
ground-truth coverage limit, not an operator failure. The Fisher-cosine
identifiability gate returns $|\cos| \geq 0.96$, indicating residual
SEI/LAM entanglement (see Limitations).

\section*{Discussion and limitations}
Conditioning the operator on an identified $\theta_{\text{BOL}}$
vector --- not raw characterisation traces --- appears to buy the
supplier-agnostic behaviour: each supplier enters only through
physically meaningful degradation parameters, so unseen suppliers land
in the same latent space as familiar ones. The censored-loss
formulation, to our knowledge novel in operator-learning for battery
ageing, lets short right-censored trajectories contribute to training
(51\% of our corpus) without biasing toward early truncation. Limitations: in-distribution val ($5.0$\,pp) exceeds held-out RMSE
because per-sample gradient noise pins training loss (batched retrain
queued); Fisher-cosine $|\cos| \geq 0.96$ shows SEI/LAM entanglement;
cross-supplier claim rests on 7 anchors.
