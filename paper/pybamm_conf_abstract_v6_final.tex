\begin{center}
{\bfseries\large One-hour characterisation to full state-of-health trajectory:\\
a supplier-agnostic PyBaMM-trained neural operator for second-life LFP RUL}\\[0.5em]
\underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India}\\
krishna.vaddepally@turno.club
\end{center}
\vspace{0.3em}

Second-life reuse of used lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells is bottlenecked
by characterisation cost: reliable remaining-useful-life (RUL) estimation
today requires months of accelerated cycling per cell, and existing empirical
models rarely transfer between supplier chemistries. We present a
supplier-agnostic neural operator that maps a single one-hour
characterisation snapshot (OCV, HPPC, GITT and a partial RPT) to the full
state-of-health (SoH) trajectory to end-of-life.

The operator is trained on a PyBaMM~[1] simulation corpus of 490
degradation trajectories, generated by perturbing a five-parameter
degradation model around seven cells drawn from three commercial LFP
suppliers (CALB, EVE, REPT). Per-cell electrochemistry is identified from
the characterisation snapshot; the operator itself is a
$\theta$-conditioned Neural ODE~[2] that takes the identified parameters
as input and returns SoH vs cycle to the end-of-life threshold. A
censored-likelihood loss makes it possible to use horizon-truncated
simulations (about half of the corpus) as training signal.

We validate the trained operator on three held-out cells --- one per
supplier --- with no per-cell degradation fit, using only a
supplier-typical parameter prior. In-window SoH errors on the actual
(nameplate-normalised) scale are 0.53, 1.10 and 0.97 percentage points
for CALB\_0029, EVE\_0003 and REPT\_0031 respectively (Figure~\ref{fig:heldout}),
well below the 3.0\,pp acceptance threshold we set from first principles.
Crucially, the standard deviation of per-cell error across the three
suppliers is only 0.24\,pp: for a fixed operator, prediction accuracy is
essentially chemistry-independent across CALB, EVE and REPT LFP variants.

The supplier-agnostic behaviour appears to come from conditioning on
identified electrochemical parameters rather than raw characterisation
traces: every supplier enters the operator through the same physical
channel. Expanding the anchor pool and per-anchor batched training are
the natural next steps.
