\begin{center}
{\bfseries\large Adaptive State-of-Health Forecasting for Second-Life LFP Cells\\
Using Physics-Informed Synthetic Data and Hybrid Model Selection}\\[0.30em]
\underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India} \textbullet\
krishna.vaddepally@turno.club\\[0.10em]
{\footnotesize Theme: Data-driven Methods \textbullet\ Presentation: Oral (poster acceptable)}
\end{center}

Second-life reuse of retired lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells requires
fast prediction of future degradation for each incoming cell. Instead of
assuming a single degradation model is optimal for all cells, we develop
an adaptive forecasting framework that selects between simple empirical
extrapolation and a PyBaMM-trained neural operator based on early-cycle
degradation complexity.

\textbf{Approach (Figure~\ref{fig:selector}).}
For each target cell the pipeline uses: (1) a short diagnostic
characterisation together with the first $K{=}50$ operating cycles as
early-cycle context; (2) a PyBaMM~[1] SPMe corpus on Prada2013 LFP
chemistry~[2] with perturbed degradation trajectories around seven anchor
cells from three suppliers (CALB, EVE, REPT), providing training
trajectories that cover the second-life SoH range; (3) a
Neural ODE~[3] operator trained using PyBaMM-generated degradation
trajectories and degradation-model-derived features, forecasting future
SoH from the diagnostic features and the $K{=}50$ context window; (4) an exponential
$SoH(n){=}a{+}b\,e^{-cn}$ baseline fitted on the same context window; and
(5) a residual-based selector that predicts the last 20\% of the context
from a fit on the first 80\% and picks the neural operator whenever the
context-holdout residual exceeds a predeclared threshold $\tau$, chosen
on a synthetic-only selector pool disjoint from the external cells.

\textbf{Results.}
Across three external LFP cells excluded from operator training
(CALB\_0029, EVE\_0003 and REPT\_0028), exponential forecasting achieved
0.70~pp mean RMSE, while the PyBaMM-trained neural operator achieved
0.80~pp. The context-residual selector combined their complementary
strengths, reducing mean RMSE to 0.47~pp and reproducing the oracle
choice on all three evaluated cells: exponential forecasting for
CALB\_0029 and REPT\_0028, and the neural operator for EVE\_0003. These
results motivate adaptive model selection rather than committing to
either forecasting method alone.

Ablations at matched split and seed indicate that PyBaMM-generated
trajectories provide useful training diversity, while explicit
degradation-parameter conditioning provides limited additional benefit
(full-, no- and reduced-$\theta$ operators: 0.80, 0.82 and 0.90~pp mean).

The current evaluation is limited to three external cells, and
longer-horizon projections require additional experimental validation.
