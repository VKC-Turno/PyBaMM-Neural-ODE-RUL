\begin{center}
{\bfseries\large Adaptive State-of-Health Forecasting for Second-Life LFP Cells\\
Using Physics-Informed Synthetic Data and Hybrid Model Selection}\\[0.30em]
\underline{Krishna Chaitanya Vaddepally}\\
\emph{Turno (Blubble Pvt.\ Ltd.), Bengaluru, India} \textbullet\
krishna.vaddepally@turno.club\\[0.10em]
{\footnotesize Theme: Data-driven Methods \textbullet\ Presentation: Oral (poster acceptable)}
\end{center}

Second-life reuse of retired lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells requires
fast prediction of future degradation for each incoming cell. Instead of
assuming a single degradation model is optimal for all cells, we develop
an adaptive forecasting framework that selects between simple empirical
extrapolation and a PyBaMM-trained neural operator based on early-cycle
degradation complexity.

\textbf{Approach (Figure~\ref{fig:selector}).}
A short diagnostic characterisation and the first $K{=}50$ operating
cycles provide early-cycle context. A Neural ODE~[3] is trained on
PyBaMM~[1] SPMe degradation trajectories generated around seven anchor
cells from three LFP suppliers (CALB, EVE, REPT) on Prada2013
chemistry~[2]. In parallel, an exponential model is fitted to the same
context. A context-holdout residual, computed by fitting the first
80\% of the context and predicting the final 20\%, selects the neural
operator when the residual exceeds a threshold chosen using synthetic
trajectories only.

\textbf{Results.}
Across three external LFP cells excluded from operator training,
exponential forecasting achieved 0.70~pp mean RMSE and the
PyBaMM-trained neural operator achieved 0.80~pp. The residual-based
selector combined their complementary strengths, reducing mean RMSE to
\textbf{0.47~pp}~---~a 33\% reduction relative to the best fixed
strategy.

Matched ablations indicate that PyBaMM-generated trajectories provide
useful training diversity, while explicit degradation-parameter
conditioning adds little additional benefit (0.80, 0.82 and 0.90~pp
mean RMSE for full-, no- and reduced-$\theta$ operators).

The current evaluation is limited to three external cells, and
longer-horizon projections require additional experimental validation.
