\section{Validation protocol}
\label{sec:validation}

To avoid optimistic bias, model calibration and validation must be separated
by either:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{cell hold-out:} leave-one-cell-out validation when multiple
  cells are available, or
  \item \textbf{protocol hold-out:} fit to a subset of characterisation tests
  and validate on protocols not used in fitting.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Key limitation for \RUL-to-EOL claims.}
If the available long-term cycling data does not reach the end-of-life
definition (e.g., 80\% \SOH), then \RUL-to-EOL accuracy cannot be directly
validated. In that case, results must be stated as within-observation-horizon
\SOH forecasting, with explicit uncertainty bounds and failure cases.
